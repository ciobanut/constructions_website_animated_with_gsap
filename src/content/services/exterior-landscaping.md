In Iraq's climate, the outdoor space is a sanctuary — a retreat from the intensity of urban life and the heat of the day. We design and construct exterior environments that are as refined as the interiors they surround. From sprawling gardens in Erbil's green suburbs to rooftop oases in Baghdad's high-rise penthouses, every landscape we create is an invitation to dwell outdoors in comfort and style.

Our landscaping philosophy treats the exterior as an extension of the interior — the same attention to materiality, proportion, and atmosphere applies. We source mature olive and citrus trees, drought-tolerant specimen planting, and natural stone from Iraqi and Turkish quarries. Water features — from reflecting pools to cascading fountains — are designed to cool the air and soothe the spirit, with recirculating systems that minimize consumption in a water-conscious region.

Hardscaping is executed with architectural precision. We build terraces in natural limestone and porcelain pavers, pathways that trace graceful arcs through formal gardens, and pool surrounds finished in non-slip travertine. Outdoor kitchens, fire pits, and lounging areas are wired for audio-visual systems and ambient lighting, creating environments that come alive at dusk. Perimeter walls and gates are designed as architectural features in their own right — in forged iron, stone, or rendered masonry.

Every landscape is engineered for sustainability. Automated irrigation with weather-responsive controllers, drainage systems that prevent waterlogging during the rainy season, and planting schemes that thrive with minimal intervention. Our after-care program ensures that gardens mature gracefully, with seasonal maintenance visits that keep every landscape at its peak. The result is an exterior that is not merely an accessory to the building, but an integral part of the living experience.
